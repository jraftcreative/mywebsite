The Challenge

You're Either Visible to AI — Or You Don't Exist

Most Singapore businesses have no idea whether AI recommends them. They've never opened ChatGPT, Gemini, or Perplexity and tested what happens when someone asks for a recommendation in their industry. The result is a blind spot that grows more expensive every month as AI adoption accelerates. As we explain in our guide on why AI search matters for Singapore businesses, if you haven't tested your AI visibility, the honest answer is: you probably don't have any.

AI visibility is fundamentally different from Google visibility. There's no "page two" of ChatGPT — your brand is either named in the answer or it's absent entirely. The overlap between Google rankings and AI citations sits below 20%, which means your SEO investment alone isn't protecting you. Businesses ranking #1 on Google are routinely absent from AI recommendations because AI platforms evaluate completely different signals: entity clarity, structured data quality, third-party authority, and content freshness.

Singapore has the second highest AI adoption rate globally at 60.9%. Consumers here are already using ChatGPT and Gemini to find restaurants, agencies, accountants, and service providers. AI recommendations convert at 4.4x the rate of traditional search clicks because users trust the curated answer. Businesses that measure and optimise their AI visibility now will compound that advantage as AI usage continues to grow. Those that wait will find themselves competing for a shrinking share of traditional search traffic. A dedicated GEO agency can help you close the gap.

Self-Assessment

How to Measure Your AI Visibility — A Self-Assessment

Before investing in AI visibility, you need to know where you stand. Here's a practical self-assessment you can run in 15 minutes.

  1. Test ChatGPT — Ask "best [your industry] in Singapore" and "recommend [your service] near [your area]." Record whether your brand appears.
  2. Test Gemini — Repeat the same prompts in Google Gemini. Compare results with ChatGPT — different platforms often recommend different brands.
  3. Test Perplexity — Perplexity uses real-time web search. If your brand appears here but not in ChatGPT, you have strong web signals but weak training data presence.
  4. Check Google AI Overviews — Search your target keywords on Google. Does an AI Overview appear? Is your brand cited in it?
  5. Benchmark competitors — For each prompt, note which 3-4 brands AI recommends instead of yours. These are your direct AI competitors.
  6. Score yourself — Count how many prompts out of 10 return your brand. 0-2: critical visibility gap. 3-5: moderate gap. 6-8: good foundation. 9-10: strong AI presence.

Most Singapore businesses score 0-2 on their first test. If that's you, it means the foundation for AI visibility hasn't been built yet — not that your business isn't good enough.

6-8 Prompts (Good Foundation)

9-10 Prompts (Strong Presence)

FAQ

AI Visibility Questions Answered

What is AI visibility?

AI visibility measures how often and how prominently your brand appears when people ask AI platforms like ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and Copilot questions related to your industry. It's the AI equivalent of search engine visibility — but instead of ranking in a list of links, your brand is either named in the answer or it isn't.

How do I check my AI visibility?

Start by asking ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity the same questions your customers would ask — "best [your service] in Singapore," "recommended [your industry] companies," or "who should I hire for [your offering]." If your brand doesn't appear in the answers, you have an AI visibility gap. We run comprehensive audits across 20-30 prompts to give you a detailed baseline.

Does Google ranking affect AI visibility?

Less than you'd expect. The overlap between Google rankings and AI citations has dropped below 20%. Ranking on page one of Google does not mean AI will recommend you. AI platforms weigh different signals — entity clarity, structured data, content freshness, and third-party authority — so a dedicated AI visibility strategy is essential.

Which AI platforms matter most for visibility?

The five platforms that matter most right now are ChatGPT, Google Gemini, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, and Microsoft Copilot. In Singapore, AI adoption sits at 60.9% and growing, so visibility across all five platforms is critical to capturing the full spectrum of AI-driven search behaviour.

How do I improve my AI visibility?

Improving AI visibility requires a combination of structured data markup, entity optimisation, citation-ready content, and third-party authority building. Each AI platform weighs these signals differently, so a cross-platform strategy is essential. Most businesses see measurable improvements within 30 to 90 days of starting a GEO programme.
