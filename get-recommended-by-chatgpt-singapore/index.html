Diagnostic

3 Reasons ChatGPT Ignores Your Brand

If ChatGPT doesn't recommend your business, it's usually one of three problems. Here's how to diagnose which one is holding you back.

1. Your Entity Data Is Missing or Inconsistent

ChatGPT can't recommend a brand it can't identify. If your business lacks structured data (JSON-LD schema), doesn't have consistent naming across directories, or has no llms.txt file, ChatGPT has no reliable entity to reference. Fix this first — it's the foundation everything else builds on.

2. Your Content Isn't Extractable

ChatGPT needs content it can parse into clear answers. Marketing copy that talks around your services without stating them directly, pages without FAQ sections, and content buried in PDFs or images are invisible to AI. Your content needs answer-first paragraphs, comparison data, and structured Q&A that ChatGPT can extract and cite.

3. Your Third-Party Signals Are Too Weak

ChatGPT cross-references your brand against independent sources before recommending you. If you have fewer than 10 reviews on Google, no presence on industry directories, and no mentions in authoritative publications, ChatGPT doesn't have enough signal to cite you with confidence. Building authority across the sources ChatGPT trusts is what tips the scale. Our LLMO services and AI visibility programmes are designed to build exactly these signals.

Before: A Singapore professional services firm had zero ChatGPT mentions. No schema markup, 3 Google reviews, inconsistent business name across directories.

After: Following 90 days of GEO — JSON-LD schema, llms.txt, 30+ reviews, directory consistency, citation-ready content — ChatGPT began recommending the firm for 6 out of 10 target prompts.

FAQ

ChatGPT Recommendation Questions Answered

Why doesn't ChatGPT recommend my business?

ChatGPT doesn't use Google rankings to decide which brands to recommend. It relies on entity clarity, structured data, content quality, and third-party mentions across the web. If your brand lacks these signals, ChatGPT simply won't know enough about you to recommend you confidently.

How does ChatGPT decide which brands to recommend?

ChatGPT selects brands based on how clearly your business entity is defined across the web, the quality and recency of content mentioning your brand, and the strength of third-party authority signals like reviews, directories, and industry publications. It synthesises all of these signals to determine which brands are most credible for a given query.

Can I pay to be recommended by ChatGPT?

There is no paid advertising programme within ChatGPT's recommendation engine. You cannot buy placements the way you can with Google Ads. The only way to get recommended is to strengthen the organic signals ChatGPT uses — entity data, structured markup, authoritative content, and consistent third-party mentions.

How long does it take to get recommended by ChatGPT?

Most businesses begin seeing improvements in ChatGPT citation rates within 30 to 90 days of implementing a GEO strategy. Quick wins like schema markup and entity optimisation can show results within weeks, while authority building compounds over several months.

Do you guarantee ChatGPT recommendations?

No ethical agency can guarantee a specific ChatGPT recommendation because the model updates its training data and behaviour regularly. What we guarantee is a systematic, data-driven approach that maximises your likelihood of being cited. We track citation rates across prompts monthly so you can see measurable progress.
