The Challenge

Your Competitors Are Posting Daily — Are You?

Singapore's F&B industry lives and dies on social media. With over 7,000 food establishments competing for attention, the brands that post consistently, create compelling video content, and engage with their community are the ones that stay booked, ordered from, and talked about.

Most F&B business owners know social media matters, but execution falls short. Inconsistent posting, rushed photos taken on a phone between orders, and generic captions don't cut it when your competitors are investing in professional food content, Reels, and TikToks that get thousands of views.

The challenge is even greater for F&B brands managing multiple channels. Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, and Google Business Profile each require different content formats, posting frequencies, and engagement strategies. Without a dedicated team handling your social media, something always slips through the cracks.

FAQ

F&B Social Media Questions Answered

How much does social media marketing cost for an F&B business in Singapore?

Our F&B social media packages start at $2,500 SGD per month for 8 posts. The Professional package at $3,500/month includes 12 posts with Reels and stories, while the Full-Service package at $5,000+/month covers 20 posts with video production and influencer collaborations.

Which social media platforms are best for F&B businesses in Singapore?

Instagram and TikTok are essential for F&B businesses — food content performs exceptionally well on both platforms. Facebook remains important for events and an older demographic, while Google Business Profile posts support local SEO. We recommend managing at least 2–3 platforms for maximum visibility.

Can you create video content for food and beverage brands?

Yes. Video is the highest-performing format for F&B content on social media. We produce Instagram Reels, TikTok videos, and story content featuring recipe reveals, behind-the-scenes kitchen footage, menu launches, and food preparation sequences that drive engagement and shares.

How do you handle seasonal menu launches on social media?

We plan content calendars 4–6 weeks ahead so seasonal launches are supported with teaser content, launch-day posts, stories, and follow-up content. Whether it's a Chinese New Year menu, Ramadan specials, or a summer drinks range, your new offerings get the social media attention they deserve.

Can social media help promote food delivery for my F&B business?

Yes. We create dedicated delivery-focused content including promo code posts, delivery menu highlights, and order-now call-to-action stories. Social media is one of the most effective channels for driving direct delivery orders and reducing dependence on third-party platform commissions.
