The Challenge

Your Website Is Failing on Mobile

Over 70% of web traffic in Singapore comes from mobile devices, yet many business websites still look broken on a phone screen. Text that's too small to read, buttons that are impossible to tap, images that overflow off-screen, and horizontal scrolling that frustrates every visitor. These are not minor annoyances — they are costing you customers every day.

Google now uses mobile-first indexing, which means it evaluates the mobile version of your site first when deciding where to rank you in search results. If your website doesn't deliver a fast, usable experience on a smartphone, your rankings will suffer — regardless of how good your desktop version looks. A site that isn't responsive is a site that's invisible.

Poor mobile UX drives bounce rates above 60% and kills conversions. Visitors who struggle with your navigation, wait too long for pages to load, or can't complete a form on their phone will leave and go to a competitor who made the effort to get responsive design right.

FAQ

Responsive Web Design Questions Answered

What is responsive web design and why does it matter in Singapore?

Responsive web design means your website automatically adapts its layout, images, and navigation to fit any screen size — from smartphones and tablets to desktop monitors. In Singapore, over 70% of web traffic comes from mobile devices. A responsive site ensures every visitor gets a fast, usable experience regardless of how they access your site, and it is required for strong Google rankings under mobile-first indexing.

How much does a responsive website cost in Singapore?

Responsive web design in Singapore typically costs between $2,500 and $10,000+ depending on the number of pages and features. Our Starter package begins at $2,500 SGD for a 5-page mobile-first website, while Business and Premium packages include more pages, custom breakpoints, CMS integration, and advanced performance optimisation.

Will a responsive design improve my Google rankings?

Yes. Google uses mobile-first indexing, which means it primarily evaluates the mobile version of your website when determining search rankings. A responsive website that loads fast, passes Core Web Vitals, and delivers a strong mobile experience will rank higher than a desktop-only or poorly optimised site. Every responsive website we build is designed to meet Google's performance and usability standards.

How do you test responsiveness across different devices?

We test every website across a comprehensive device matrix that includes iPhones, Android phones, iPads, Android tablets, laptops, and desktop monitors. We verify layouts at every common breakpoint — 320px, 375px, 414px, 768px, 1024px, 1440px, and above. We also run automated testing for touch target sizing, font readability, and Core Web Vitals scores on both mobile and desktop.

Can you make my existing website responsive?

Yes. We offer responsive retrofits for existing websites that are not mobile-friendly. Depending on your current site's codebase, we either restructure your CSS and layout system to be fluid and adaptive, or recommend a rebuild if the existing code would limit performance. We will assess your site and recommend the most cost-effective approach during a free consultation.
