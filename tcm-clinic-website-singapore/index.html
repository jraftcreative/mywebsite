The Challenge

Patients Are Searching for TCM Practitioners Online

Traditional Chinese Medicine is deeply rooted in Singapore's healthcare landscape. From neighbourhood TCM clinics in Chinatown and Toa Payoh to modern integrative wellness centres in Orchard and Raffles Place, thousands of Singaporeans rely on acupuncture, herbal medicine, and tuina for their health. But most TCM clinics still depend on walk-ins and word-of-mouth — missing the growing number of patients who search online first.

Younger Singaporeans and expats increasingly turn to Google when looking for a TCM practitioner. They compare treatment options, check practitioner credentials, read reviews, and want to book appointments without making a phone call. If your clinic doesn't have a professional website — or worse, has an outdated one buried on page three of search results — those patients are booking with a competitor instead.

TCM clinics also face unique challenges online. Patients may not understand the difference between acupuncture, herbal prescriptions, and tuina massage. Your website needs to educate, build trust, and clearly present your practitioner's TCMPB registration — all while complying with MOH advertising regulations for traditional medicine services in Singapore.

FAQ

TCM Website Design Questions Answered

How much does a TCM clinic website cost in Singapore?

A professional TCM clinic website in Singapore typically costs between $2,500 and $10,000+ depending on features. Our Starter package at $2,500 SGD includes a 5-page site with treatment pages, practitioner profile, and contact form. Business and Premium packages add online appointment booking, bilingual content, herb catalogues, and multi-location support.

Can you build a bilingual website in English and Chinese for my TCM clinic?

Yes. Many TCM clinics in Singapore serve both English-speaking and Mandarin-speaking patients. Our Business package ($5,500) and above include full bilingual support — with properly translated content, language toggle functionality, and SEO optimisation for both English and Chinese search terms.

Do TCM clinic websites need to comply with any regulations in Singapore?

Yes. The Traditional Chinese Medicine Practitioners Board (TCMPB) under MOH regulates TCM advertising in Singapore. Your website must not make unsubstantiated medical claims, and practitioners listed must be TCMPB-registered. We ensure all website content meets these compliance requirements.

Can patients book TCM appointments online through the website?

Yes. Our Business package ($5,500) and above include online appointment booking integration. Patients can select their preferred practitioner, choose a treatment type such as acupuncture or herbal consultation, pick a time slot, and receive automated confirmation — making it easier for patients and reducing your clinic's admin workload.

How long does it take to build a TCM clinic website?

Most TCM clinic websites go live within 2–3 weeks. A standard 5-page site can be ready in 2 weeks. Bilingual websites or projects with custom features like herb databases or multi-location pages may take 3–4 weeks. We provide a clear timeline before starting.
