Your potential clients are scrolling past beauty content every day, but organic posts from business pages now reach less than 5% of your followers. The beauty industry thrives on visual content, and Meta's ad platforms are purpose-built for it. You need campaigns designed specifically for beauty businesses in the Singapore market.

FAQ

Facebook Ads for Beauty Salons — Questions Answered

How much do Facebook Ads cost for beauty salons in Singapore?

Most beauty salons start with a monthly ad spend of $800–$2,500 on top of management fees. Facebook and Instagram ads for beauty services typically cost $0.30–$1.50 per click in Singapore, making them more affordable than Google Ads while offering powerful visual targeting options.

Should my beauty salon advertise on Facebook or Instagram?

Both. Instagram is ideal for showcasing treatments through visual content, Reels, and Stories, while Facebook excels at reaching a broader demographic including working professionals and mothers. We run campaigns across both platforms from a single Meta Ads Manager to maximise your reach and bookings.

What kind of ad creatives work best for beauty salons?

Before/after transformation content, short treatment process videos, client testimonials, and limited-time promotional offers consistently perform best for beauty salons in Singapore. We create thumb-stopping visuals and compelling copy that drive appointment bookings.

How do you target the right audience for my beauty salon?

We use a combination of location targeting (within 3–5 km of your salon), interest-based targeting (beauty, skincare, self-care), demographic targeting (age, gender, income), and lookalike audiences built from your existing client base. This ensures your ads reach people most likely to book.

Can Facebook Ads drive walk-in appointments for my salon?

Yes. We run store visit and local awareness campaigns that target users within walking distance of your salon. Combined with compelling offers like first-visit discounts or same-day availability, these campaigns drive spontaneous bookings and walk-in traffic — particularly effective in high-footfall areas like Orchard and Bugis.
