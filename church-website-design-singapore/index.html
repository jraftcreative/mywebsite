The Challenge

Your Members Are Looking Online — What Do They Find?

Too many churches in Singapore still rely on Facebook groups and WhatsApp broadcasts as their primary digital presence. New visitors searching "church near Jurong" or "Sunday service times Singapore" land on outdated directory listings or social media pages with no clear information. When someone is looking for a place of worship, a scattered online presence makes it harder for them to take that first step through your doors.

Then there is the practical side. Members want to re-watch last week's sermon, sign up for a retreat, or give their tithes online — but without a proper website, each of these tasks becomes a separate chain of messages to the church office. Volunteers spend hours fielding the same questions about service times, event details, and giving instructions that a well-built website could answer in seconds.

Churches also face unique communication needs. You are not selling a product — you are building a community. Your website needs to reflect your church's heart and mission while making it genuinely easy for newcomers to learn about your beliefs, find a service, and connect with a ministry. A generic business template will not do that justice. Your church needs a website built with purpose.

What We Build

What We Build for Churches & Ministries

Every church website we deliver is designed around the way congregations actually operate in Singapore. We go beyond generic templates — your site will have sermon archives, event registration, online giving, and ministry pages that help your church stay connected throughout the week, not just on Sunday.

We also know that churches need more than a website alone. Sermon Archives & Media

Give your congregation access to past sermons organised by date, speaker, series, or Scripture reference. We build media pages that support audio playback, video embeds, and downloadable sermon notes — so members can revisit teachings anytime.

Event Calendar & Registration

List upcoming services, retreats, prayer meetings, camps, and community events in one central calendar. Members can view details and register online — cutting down on manual sign-ups and giving your admin team a clear headcount.

Online Giving & Donations

Make tithing and offerings simple with secure online giving pages. We integrate payment options including credit card, PayNow, and bank transfer — with support for one-time gifts, recurring giving, and designated funds for missions or building projects.

Ministry & Community Pages

Showcase your church's ministries — youth, children, worship, small groups, missions — with dedicated pages that explain what each ministry does, when they meet, and how to get involved. Help newcomers find their place in your community.

FAQ

Church Website Design Questions Answered

How much does a church website cost in Singapore?

A professional church website in Singapore typically costs between $2,500 and $10,000+ depending on features. Our Starter package at $2,500 SGD includes a 5-page site with service times, contact form, and mobile-responsive design. Business and Premium packages add sermon archives, event calendars, online giving, member portals, and multi-campus support.

Can you add online giving and donations to the website?

Yes. Our Business package ($5,500) and above include online giving integration. We set up secure donation pages that support one-time and recurring giving via credit card, PayNow, or bank transfer — making it easy for members to tithe and give offerings from any device.

Will the website include a sermon archive?

Yes. We build sermon archive pages that organise recordings by date, speaker, series, or Scripture reference. Members can listen to audio, watch video, and download sermon notes. Our Business package includes the full sermon archive system, and Premium adds live streaming integration.

Can you build an event calendar with registration?

Absolutely. We create event calendar pages where your church can list upcoming services, retreats, camps, prayer meetings, and community events. Members can view event details and register online — reducing manual sign-ups and giving your admin team a clear attendee list.

How long does it take to build a church website?

Most church websites go live within 2-3 weeks. A straightforward 5-page site can be ready in as little as 2 weeks, while larger projects with sermon archives, online giving, event registration, or multi-campus features may take 3-4 weeks. We provide a clear timeline before starting work.
