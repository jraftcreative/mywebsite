The Challenge

Outdated Websites Undermine Corporate Authority

Investors, partners, job seekers, and regulators all form impressions of your company within seconds of landing on your website. An outdated design, inconsistent branding, or clunky navigation tells stakeholders that your organisation is behind the times — regardless of how strong your actual operations are.

Many corporate websites in Singapore were built years ago and never updated to reflect the company's current size, capabilities, or market positioning. Key pages are buried. Investor information is hard to find. Careers sections look like afterthoughts. The result is a poor stakeholder experience that erodes confidence and costs you talent, partnerships, and deal flow.

A modern corporate website needs to serve multiple audiences simultaneously — shareholders reviewing financials, prospective employees evaluating culture, clients assessing capability, and media seeking company information. Generic templates cannot handle this complexity. You need a purpose-built site architecture designed around your organisation's structure and stakeholder priorities.

FAQ

Corporate Website Design Questions Answered

How much does a corporate website cost in Singapore?

A professional corporate website in Singapore typically costs between $2,500 and $10,000+ depending on scope. Our Starter package at $2,500 SGD covers a 5-page site with brand-led design, leadership profiles, and contact form. Business and Premium packages add investor relations sections, careers portals, and multi-department architectures.

Can you build an investor relations section on our corporate website?

Yes. Our Business and Premium packages include dedicated investor relations pages with annual report hosting, financial highlights, corporate governance disclosures, and shareholder information. We structure these sections for clarity and compliance with SGX listing requirements where applicable.

Do you build careers portals for corporate websites?

Yes. We design recruitment pages and careers portals that showcase your company culture, list open positions, and integrate with applicant tracking systems. The goal is to attract top talent while reinforcing your employer brand across every touchpoint.

How do you handle multi-department content on a corporate website?

We design scalable site architectures with clear navigation hierarchies, department-specific landing pages, and consistent branding across all sections. Each department gets its own content area while the overarching corporate identity stays unified throughout the site.

How long does it take to build a corporate website?

Most corporate websites go live within 3-4 weeks. A 5-page site can be ready in 2 weeks, while larger projects with investor relations sections, careers portals, or multi-language support may take 4-6 weeks. We provide a detailed timeline and milestones before starting.
