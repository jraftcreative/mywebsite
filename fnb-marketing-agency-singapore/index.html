The Challenge

Running a Food Business Is Hard — Marketing Shouldn't Be

Singapore's F&B industry is worth over $12 billion, but competition has never been fiercer. New restaurants, cafes, and food brands launch every week, all fighting for the same customers. The ones that survive long-term aren't just the best cooks — they're the best marketers.

Most F&B business owners are stretched thin. Between managing kitchen operations, staff, suppliers, and food costs, there's no bandwidth to also run Google Ads, post on Instagram, write blog content, manage reviews, and optimise for SEO. Hiring one freelancer for social media and another for ads creates disjointed marketing with no coherent strategy.

That's why F&B businesses increasingly turn to specialist marketing agencies. A single partner who understands the food industry, manages every digital channel, and ensures everything works together as one system — from the first Google search to the fifth visit. That's what we do.

FAQ

F&B Marketing Agency Questions Answered

How much does an F&B marketing agency charge in Singapore?

F&B marketing agency fees in Singapore start at $3,000 SGD per month for essential digital marketing. Growth retainers at $5,500/month include full-service marketing across SEO, ads, and social media. Enterprise retainers at $10,000+/month cover multi-location campaigns with a dedicated strategist.

What services does an F&B marketing agency provide?

As a full-service F&B marketing agency, we provide website design and development, SEO and local search optimisation, Google Ads and Meta Ads management, social media content and management, branding and identity design, content marketing, email marketing, and review management — all tailored specifically for food and beverage businesses.

Why hire a specialist F&B marketing agency instead of a general agency?

F&B marketing has unique requirements: food photography, menu-driven SEO, review management, delivery platform competition, and seasonal promotions. A specialist agency already understands these challenges, knows what works in Singapore's F&B market, and doesn't need months to learn your industry.

How do you measure success for F&B marketing?

We track metrics that directly impact your revenue: reservations, online orders, foot traffic trends, Google Maps actions (calls, directions, website visits), cost per acquisition, and marketing ROI. Every metric is reported clearly in regular performance reports — no vanity numbers.

Can you handle marketing for F&B franchises and multi-outlet groups?

Yes. Multi-outlet and franchise F&B marketing is a core strength. We create brand-consistent marketing across all locations while adapting strategy for each outlet's neighbourhood and audience. Each location gets its own Google Business Profile, localised ads, and location-specific content.
