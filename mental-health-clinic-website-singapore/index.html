The Challenge

People Seeking Help Start Their Search Online

Mental health awareness in Singapore has grown significantly in recent years. More Singaporeans are seeking professional support for anxiety, depression, relationship issues, and workplace stress than ever before. But for many, the first step is not a phone call — it's a Google search. They look for therapists, compare credentials, read about different approaches, and try to find someone they feel comfortable opening up to — all from the privacy of their phone or laptop.

This is where most mental health practices fall short online. Many clinics in Singapore either have no website at all, or have one that feels clinical, impersonal, or outdated. In a field where trust and comfort are everything, a poorly designed website can turn away someone who is already hesitant about seeking help. Your website needs to feel approachable, professional, and safe — from the moment a visitor lands on it.

Mental health clinics face unique design challenges that general web agencies often overlook. The tone has to be warm without being patronising. Service descriptions need to explain therapy approaches without clinical jargon. Practitioner credentials — SMC registration for psychiatrists, SRP registration for psychologists — must be clearly displayed. And every element of the user experience needs to reinforce confidentiality, from the booking form to the privacy policy.

What We Build

What We Build for Mental Health Clinics

Every mental health website we deliver is designed with sensitivity and purpose. We go beyond generic healthcare templates — your site will feature service pages that explain each therapy modality clearly, practitioner profiles that build trust, confidential online booking, and self-assessment tools that guide visitors toward taking the first step.

Your online presence extends beyond your website. Pair your new site with SEO services to rank for searches like "therapist near me" and specific mental health conditions, social media marketing to share mental wellness content and reduce stigma, and paid ads management to reach people actively searching for professional support in Singapore.

Service Pages by Therapy Type

Dedicated pages for counselling, psychotherapy, psychiatry, couples therapy, cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT), and more. Each page explains the approach in accessible language, describes who it helps, what a typical session looks like, and guides visitors toward booking a consultation.

Therapist & Psychologist Profiles

Detailed profile pages for each practitioner — qualifications, SMC or SRP registration, areas of specialisation, therapeutic modalities, languages spoken, and a personal statement. Potential clients want to know who they'll be speaking with, and these profiles help them find the right fit.

Confidential Online Booking

A secure, private booking system that lets clients schedule appointments without calling. They can select a therapist, choose a session type, pick a convenient time, and provide only the information needed — with SSL encryption and clear privacy assurances throughout the process.

Insurance Panels & Self-Assessment Tools

Clear insurance panel information showing which plans your clinic accepts — including coverage details for major insurers in Singapore. Plus, validated self-assessment screening tools like the PHQ-9 and GAD-7, presented with clinical disclaimers, that guide visitors toward professional consultation.

FAQ

Mental Health Website Design Questions Answered

How much does a mental health clinic website cost in Singapore?

A professional mental health clinic website in Singapore typically costs between $2,500 and $10,000+ depending on features. Our Starter package at $2,500 SGD includes a 5-page site with service pages, therapist profile, and contact form. Business and Premium packages add confidential online booking, self-assessment tools, insurance panel information, and multi-location support.

How do you handle confidentiality on mental health websites?

Confidentiality is central to every mental health website we build. We use SSL encryption across the entire site, build discreet contact forms that don't expose sensitive information, and design the booking process to be private and secure. We also ensure your privacy policy clearly communicates how patient data is handled in compliance with the PDPA.

Can you build therapist and psychologist profile pages?

Yes. We build detailed profile pages for each practitioner — including their qualifications, registration status with the Singapore Medical Council (SMC) or Singapore Register of Psychologists (SRP), areas of specialisation, therapeutic approach, and a professional bio. These profiles help potential clients find the right therapist for their needs.

Can you include self-assessment tools on the website?

Yes. Our Business package ($5,500) and above can include validated self-assessment screening tools — such as the PHQ-9 for depression or GAD-7 for anxiety. These are presented with appropriate clinical disclaimers and guide visitors toward booking a professional consultation based on their responses.

How long does it take to build a mental health clinic website?

Most mental health clinic websites go live within 2–3 weeks. A standard 5-page site can be ready in 2 weeks. Larger projects with self-assessment tools, therapist directories, or multi-location features may take 3–4 weeks. We provide a clear timeline before starting.
