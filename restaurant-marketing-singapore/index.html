The Challenge

Great Food Deserves Customers Who Know About It

Singapore has over 7,000 restaurants, and new ones open every week. Word-of-mouth and walk-in traffic aren't enough to sustain a restaurant business anymore. The majority of dining decisions now start on Google, Instagram, or TikTok — and the restaurants that dominate those channels are the ones filling tables consistently.

The challenge for most restaurant owners is that digital marketing requires expertise across multiple channels: SEO to rank on Google, paid ads to capture immediate demand, social media to build a following, and content to establish authority. Managing all of this while running a restaurant is unrealistic.

That's where a full-service approach pays off. Instead of piecemealing different agencies for SEO, ads, and social media, a single marketing partner who understands the restaurant industry can build a cohesive strategy where every channel supports the others — and every dollar spent drives measurable results.

FAQ

Restaurant Marketing Questions Answered

How much does restaurant marketing cost in Singapore?

Restaurant digital marketing in Singapore starts at $3,000 SGD per month for essential services including SEO and social media. Growth packages at $5,500/month add Google Ads and content marketing. Enterprise packages at $10,000+/month include a dedicated strategist, multi-channel campaigns, and influencer partnerships.

What's included in restaurant digital marketing?

Our restaurant marketing packages combine multiple channels: SEO and Google Maps optimisation, Google Ads for immediate traffic, social media content and management, review management, and performance analytics. The specific mix depends on your package and goals.

How long before I see results from restaurant marketing?

Google Ads can drive traffic within days. Social media builds engagement within 2–4 weeks. SEO typically shows ranking improvements within 3–4 months. A multi-channel approach means you see results at different speeds — paid channels deliver quickly while organic channels build long-term.

Do you work with restaurants that have multiple outlets?

Yes. Multi-location restaurant marketing is a core capability. We create location-specific strategies, manage individual Google Business Profiles, run geo-targeted ads for each outlet, and maintain consistent branding across all locations while adapting messaging for each neighbourhood.

Can restaurant marketing reduce my dependence on delivery platforms?

Absolutely. One of the primary goals of restaurant marketing is building your own customer channels. SEO drives direct website traffic, Google Ads captures delivery searches, and social media builds a following you own. Over time, this reduces reliance on platforms that take 25–30% commission.
