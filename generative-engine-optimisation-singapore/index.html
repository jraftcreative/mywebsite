The Challenge

Google Rankings No Longer Guarantee Visibility

Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) is the emerging discipline of making your brand visible in AI-generated answers. When a consumer asks ChatGPT "best web design agency in Singapore" or asks Gemini "who should I hire for digital marketing," the AI names 3-4 brands in a direct answer. There are no page-two results, no ads above the fold, no ten blue links. Your brand is either recommended or it doesn't exist in that moment.

Traditional SEO optimises for Google's algorithm — keywords, backlinks, page speed, and technical crawlability. GEO optimises for how AI models select and cite brands — entity clarity, structured data, content freshness, and third-party authority. The overlap between Google rankings and AI citations has fallen below 20%. A business can rank #1 on Google and still be completely absent from ChatGPT's recommendations. These are fundamentally different systems requiring fundamentally different strategies. Our AI SEO services bridge both disciplines.

Singapore businesses that adopt GEO early gain a compounding advantage. With 60.9% of Singapore's population already using generative AI and AI recommendations converting at 4.4x the rate of traditional search clicks, the channel is too large and too effective to treat as experimental. Every month without a GEO strategy is a month your competitors are building the authority signals that make AI recommend them by default.

FAQ

Generative Engine Optimisation Questions Answered

What is Generative Engine Optimisation?

Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) is the practice of optimising your brand and content so that AI-powered search platforms — ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, AI Overviews, and Copilot — recommend your business in their generated answers. It focuses on the signals AI models use to select and cite brands, which differ significantly from traditional Google ranking factors.

How is GEO different from SEO?

SEO optimises for Google's algorithm to rank your website in search results. GEO optimises for how AI models decide which brands to name in generated answers. The overlap between Google rankings and AI citations is below 20%, meaning strong SEO does not automatically translate to AI visibility. GEO targets entity clarity, structured data, content structure, and third-party authority signals.

Is GEO the same as LLMO?

GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) and LLMO (Large Language Model Optimisation) refer to the same discipline — optimising your brand for AI-generated answers. GEO is the broader, more commonly adopted term that encompasses all generative AI platforms, while LLMO specifically references large language models. In practice, the strategies and outcomes are identical.

Which AI platforms does GEO target?

GEO targets all major AI-powered search platforms: ChatGPT, Google Gemini, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, and Microsoft Copilot. Each platform has different source preferences and recommendation algorithms, so an effective GEO strategy accounts for platform-specific signals while building cross-platform authority.
