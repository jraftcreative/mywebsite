The Challenge

Florists Are Losing Orders Without a Proper Online Storefront

When someone in Singapore needs flowers — a last-minute anniversary bouquet, a condolence wreath for a wake, or a CNY hamper for a client — they reach for their phone and search. If your flower shop only exists on Instagram or a marketplace listing, you are competing on someone else's platform with no control over your pricing display, delivery options, or brand presentation. The florist with a dedicated website that shows available arrangements, accepts orders online, and confirms same-day delivery wins that customer every time.

Social media and marketplace platforms like Carousell or Shopee are discovery channels, not ordering systems built for florists. They cannot handle delivery zone pricing across Singapore's postal districts, enforce same-day cut-off times, display occasion-based collections for Valentine's Day or Mother's Day, or manage recurring corporate orders. Every order placed through DMs or WhatsApp requires manual confirmation, payment follow-up, and address clarification — adding friction that costs you sales during peak periods when speed matters most.

Singapore's floral market is intensely competitive. Established chains, boutique studios, and home-based florists are all vying for the same pool of customers across the island. A purpose-built website turns your catalogue into a 24/7 ordering system — one that showcases your arrangements beautifully, handles delivery logistics automatically, and lets customers order with confidence at any hour, whether they are in Jurong, Tampines, or the CBD.

FAQ

Florist Website Design Questions Answered

How much does a florist website cost in Singapore?

A professional florist website in Singapore typically costs between $2,500 and $10,000+ depending on features. Our Starter package at $2,500 SGD includes a 5-page site with product catalogue, delivery information, and contact form. Business and Premium packages add online ordering, delivery zone logic, and subscription management.

Can customers order flowers directly through my website?

Yes. Our Business package ($5,500) and above include a full online ordering system where customers can browse arrangements, select a delivery date and time slot, enter a delivery address, and pay securely online. You receive instant order notifications and can manage everything from a simple dashboard.

Can the website handle same-day delivery orders?

Yes. We configure same-day delivery cut-off times so customers placing orders before your deadline can select same-day delivery. Orders placed after the cut-off automatically default to the next available slot. This prevents unfulfillable orders while maximising your same-day revenue.

Do you set up delivery zones on the website?

Yes. We configure delivery zones across Singapore — island-wide, CBD, specific districts, or postal code ranges — with different delivery fees for each zone. Customers enter their postal code at checkout and see the applicable delivery fee instantly, reducing abandoned carts and delivery disputes.

How long does it take to build a florist website?

Most florist websites go live within 2-3 weeks. A 5-page catalogue site can be ready in 2 weeks, while larger projects with online ordering, delivery zone logic, or subscription features may take 3-4 weeks. We provide a clear timeline before starting.
