Terminology

GEO, LLMO, AEO, AIO — What Do They All Mean?

The industry hasn't settled on a single name for optimising brands for AI search. You'll see agencies use GEO, LLMO, AEO, and AIO interchangeably — which can make it difficult to compare services or understand what you're actually buying. Here's what each term means and how they relate to each other.

GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation)

The broadest and most widely adopted term. GEO covers optimisation across all generative AI platforms — ChatGPT, Google Gemini, Perplexity, Copilot, and Google AI Overviews. It's the umbrella term that encompasses every strategy for getting your brand recommended in AI answers. This is the term Jraft Creative uses because it reflects the full scope of what we do: optimise for all generative engines, not just one platform or technology.

LLMO (Large Language Model Optimisation)

LLMO frames the same discipline from a technology angle — focusing on the large language models that power AI search. The strategies are identical to GEO; the label simply emphasises the underlying AI technology rather than the platforms. If you've been searching for LLMO services in Singapore, you're looking for the same thing.

AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation)

AEO positions AI platforms as "answer engines" rather than search engines. This framing is more common in the US market and emphasises the shift from links to direct answers. The optimisation strategies — structured data, entity clarity, authority building — are the same as GEO.

AIO (AI Overview Optimisation)

AIO specifically targets Google's AI Overviews feature — the AI-generated summaries that appear at the top of Google search results. It's the narrowest of the four terms, focusing on a single platform. Our AI SEO services include AI Overview optimisation as part of a broader multi-platform strategy.

Bottom line: GEO, LLMO, AEO, and AIO all describe the same core discipline. The strategies are identical regardless of which label you use. What matters is that your agency understands how AI decides which brands to recommend — and builds a programme around those signals.
