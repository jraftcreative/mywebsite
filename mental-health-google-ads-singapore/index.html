The Challenge

People Are Searching for Help — Can They Find Your Practice?

Mental health awareness in Singapore has reached a turning point. The Singapore Mental Health Study found that 1 in 7 people experience a mental health condition, and the stigma around seeking help is steadily declining. More Singaporeans are searching Google for "therapist near me", "anxiety counselling Singapore", and "psychologist for depression" than ever before — and that first search is often the hardest step they'll take.

If your practice doesn't appear when someone finally decides to seek help, they may delay reaching out or choose a less suitable provider. The stakes are higher than a missed appointment — it's a person in need not getting the support they deserve. Yet most digital agencies treat mental health marketing the same as any other industry, using generic tactics that feel clinical, pushy, or tone-deaf. Mental health advertising requires a fundamentally different approach.

FAQ

Mental Health Google Ads — Questions Answered

How much should mental health clinics budget for Google Ads in Singapore?

Most mental health practices start with a monthly ad spend of $1,500–$5,000 on top of management fees. Keywords like "psychologist Singapore" cost $4–$12 per click, while therapy-specific terms like "CBT therapy Singapore" or "couples counselling near me" range from $3–$8. The higher cost per click reflects the high lifetime value of each therapy client.

How do you ensure mental health ads are sensitive and appropriate?

Every element — ad copy, landing pages, and calls-to-action — is crafted with empathy. We avoid fear-based language, stigmatising terms, and high-pressure tactics. Our ads focus on creating a sense of safety and accessibility, acknowledging that searching for therapy is itself a significant step for many people.

Can Google Ads target corporate EAP contracts?

Yes. We create separate B2B campaigns targeting HR directors and business owners searching for Employee Assistance Programmes, corporate counselling, and workplace wellness services. These campaigns use different messaging and landing pages from patient-facing ads, focusing on organisational outcomes and programme structures.

What Google advertising policies apply to mental health services?

Google requires healthcare advertiser certification for mental health ads in Singapore and has policies around prohibited claims and sensitive content. We handle the full compliance process — proper certification, compliant ad copy, appropriate landing page content, and correct handling of crisis-related keywords with safety resources.

How do you protect client confidentiality in ad tracking?

Client privacy is non-negotiable. We use aggregate conversion tracking that measures campaign performance without exposing individual client data. No personally identifiable information enters Google Ads, and our tracking setup fully complies with Singapore's Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) and healthcare data standards.
