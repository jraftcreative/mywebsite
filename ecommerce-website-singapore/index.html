The Challenge

Selling Online Shouldn't Be This Complicated

Too many Singapore businesses are losing sales to clunky ecommerce platforms that weren't built with their customers in mind. A slow-loading product page, a confusing checkout flow, or a store that breaks on mobile means abandoned carts and lost revenue.

Customers expect a seamless shopping experience — browse products, add to cart, and pay in seconds. If your checkout doesn't support PayNow or GrabPay, if your product pages aren't optimised for search, or if your store takes more than three seconds to load, shoppers will go somewhere else.

Singapore's ecommerce market is growing fast, but competition is fierce. Whether you're launching a new online store, migrating from Shopify or WooCommerce, or scaling an existing operation, your ecommerce site needs to be fast, trustworthy, and built to convert — not just look good.

FAQ

Ecommerce Website Questions Answered

How much does an ecommerce website cost in Singapore?

A professional ecommerce website in Singapore typically costs between $5,500 and $15,000+ depending on features and complexity. Our Business package at $5,500 SGD includes up to 50 products, a payment gateway, and mobile-responsive design. Premium and Enterprise packages add unlimited products, multiple payment gateways, inventory management, and custom integrations.

Which payment gateways do you support?

We integrate all major payment gateways used in Singapore, including PayNow, GrabPay, Stripe, PayPal, and credit/debit card processing. Our Business package includes a single gateway, while Premium and Enterprise packages support multiple gateways so your customers can pay however they prefer.

Can you migrate my store from Shopify or WooCommerce?

Yes. We handle full migrations from Shopify, WooCommerce, Magento, and other ecommerce platforms. We transfer your product data, customer records, order history, and SEO settings so you don't lose traffic or rankings during the move. We also set up proper 301 redirects to preserve your search engine authority.

Do you handle product photography?

We partner with professional product photographers in Singapore and can coordinate a shoot as part of your project. High-quality product images are critical for ecommerce — they directly impact conversion rates. We also optimise all images for fast loading without sacrificing visual quality.

How long does it take to build an online store?

Most ecommerce websites go live within 3–5 weeks depending on the number of products and complexity. A Business-tier store with up to 50 products can be ready in 3 weeks, while Enterprise builds with custom integrations and multi-currency support typically take 5–6 weeks. We provide a clear timeline before starting.
